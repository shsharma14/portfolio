Hotel Bookings Analysis

May 20, 2021

The main idea behind this project is to optimize product price and availability for maximum revenue. So, basically the goal is to increase the average daily revenue of a hotel by selling the right product via the right distribution channel, for a reasonable price to a customer.

I analyzed a hotel dataset that consisted of around 120k bookings, with details related to bookings, customer, length of stay, travel agents, distribution channels.

Feature Engineering and Exploratory data analysis were crucial steps for this project to select important features that are affecting the target variable, Average Daily Revenue.

After that, I created linear regression models using stepwise elimination to predict what factors affect the average daily revenue generated by a hotel.

Next, I utilized support vector machine and logistic regression algorithms to determine on what factors a booking is likely to be canceled.

Lastly, I performed association rules mining to find out what specific bookings are likely to be canceled by the customer.

After data mining, I came up with the following business recommendations.

  • Cater to the Business Travel visitor type among the city and resort hotels, offering free room upgrades after a certain number of stays, and ascertain local business partnerships.
  • When expanding your hotels, focus on city locations given they generate more ADR.
  • Especially at city hotel locations, focus on Couples, and offer them room upgrades and meal vouchers.
  • Incentivize staying at hotels during the fall and summer seasons to prevent more cancellations, which could be done by recommending seasonal activities and tourist attractions.
  • Have a flexible deposit strategy so that you are more likely to gain ADR from all types of customers, instead of having most visitor types have no deposit when making reservations.
